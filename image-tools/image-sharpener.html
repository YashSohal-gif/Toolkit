About sharpening

Will this fix a very blurry photo?

No — this enhances edges in photos that are only slightly soft. A photo that's badly out of focus or heavily motion-blurred has lost detail that no filter can truly restore.

How does it work?

It uses an unsharp mask — a classic sharpening technique that boosts contrast right along detected edges, making the image look crisper without adding fake detail.

Sharpness vs. Acutance — what's the difference?

Sharpness (small radius) enhances fine, pixel-level detail — good for text and hair. Acutance (large radius) boosts contrast along broader edges instead, giving photos a punchier, more "in-focus" feel without touching fine texture. Real acutance is exactly this: edge contrast, not added detail.

What do noise reduction and film grain do?

Reduce noise softly blurs away speckly sensor noise before sharpening, so sharpening doesn't amplify it. Add film grain is the opposite — a stylistic effect that adds back a fine random texture reminiscent of film photography, applied after sharpening.

Is my photo uploaded anywhere?

No — sharpening happens entirely in your browser. Your photo never leaves your device.
